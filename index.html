Dynamical Modular Architectures
for Neuro‑Symbolic Reasoning Agents

Dominic Paragas1
1University of California, Berkeley
Paper arXiv Video Code

DMA‑NSRA organizes LLM agents as interacting dynamical modules, achieving robust neuro‑symbolic reasoning under distribution shift.

Abstract

Current large‑language‑model agents often couple brittle prompt engineering with opaque monolithic neural policies. We cast the control loop of an LLM agent as a coupled dynamical system whose nodes are symbolic (formal verifier, planner) or sub‑symbolic (LLM) modules connected by learnable interfaces. This perspective yields a family of Dynamical Modular Architectures (DMA) that enjoy three desiderata simultaneously: robustness under distribution shift, interpretability via explicit information flow, and compositional transfer between tasks.

We derive Lyapunov‑inspired stability criteria for module interaction, propose a self‑reflection scheduler that triggers symbolic sub‑routines exactly at Hopf‑bifurcation points, and demonstrate performance gains on math‑proof and scientific discovery benchmarks.
